## FAQs:
1. Q: How much do study abroad consultants charge in India?
   A: Prices range wildly. Mass-market consultants charge ₹25,000-₹75,000 for basic application help. Premium boutique firms like Crimson and Athena charge ₹3L-₹8L for comprehensive programs. Blue Ocean's signature program is ₹4.5-5.5L and includes everything from profile building to scholarship strategy. The question isn't cost but ROI. If a ₹5L investment leads to ₹1 Cr in scholarships, it pays for itself 20 times over.

2. Q: Are study abroad consultants worth it?
   A: It depends on the consultant. A good consultant with Ivy League/Oxbridge expertise can mean the difference between a rejection and a ₹1 Cr scholarship offer. A bad consultant just fills forms. The test: ask them how many students they've placed at your target university in the last 2 years. If they can't name specific students and outcomes, they're selling promises, not results.

3. Q: What is the difference between a study abroad consultant and a college counselor?
   A: A study abroad consultant typically handles logistics: university selection, application submission, visa processing. A college counselor (or admissions counselor) goes deeper: profile building, essay strategy, interview prep, scholarship matching. For elite admissions (Ivy League, Oxbridge), you need a counselor, not just a consultant. The distinction matters because the strategy behind your application matters more than the paperwork.

4. Q: How do I verify a study abroad consultant's claims?
   A: Ask for verifiable outcomes: specific student names (with permission), university names, and scholarship amounts. Check Google reviews, but also ask to speak with 2-3 past families directly. Verify the lead counselor's credentials independently. If they claim a "95% acceptance rate," ask: acceptance to which universities? Top 30 or top 300? The specificity of their answer tells you everything.
