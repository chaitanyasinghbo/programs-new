## FAQs:
1. Q: Is it easier to get into Oxford or Harvard from India?
   A: They're different systems. Oxford's overall acceptance rate (~15%) is higher than Harvard's (~3.4%), but Oxford evaluates primarily on academic strength and subject-specific aptitude through interviews and admissions tests. Harvard evaluates holistically including extracurriculars and essays. Indian students with strong academic records but fewer extracurriculars may find Oxford more accessible. Students with a strong narrative and leadership spike may find Harvard more receptive.

2. Q: Do Indian students need A-Levels for Oxford and Cambridge?
   A: No. Oxford and Cambridge accept CBSE, ICSE, and IB qualifications. For CBSE, they typically want predicted scores of 95%+ with specific subject requirements for your chosen course. IB students typically need 38-40+ points. Each college may have additional requirements. The key is checking the specific requirements for your course, as these vary significantly between subjects.

3. Q: How does UCAS work for Indian students?
   A: UCAS is the UK's centralised application platform, similar to Common App but with key differences. You apply to a maximum of 5 universities (only 4 if applying to both Oxford and Cambridge is not allowed — you can only pick one). Applications open in September and the Oxford/Cambridge deadline is October 15. Your application includes predicted grades, a personal statement, and a teacher reference. There are no separate essays for each university.

4. Q: Which is better for Indian students: UK or US universities?
   A: It depends on your profile and goals. UK degrees are typically 3 years (saving time and money). US degrees offer more flexibility and broader liberal arts education. UK admissions are more academic-focused. US admissions value extracurriculars heavily. For scholarships, the US generally offers more financial aid to international students. Many of our students apply to both and decide based on offers and financial aid packages.
