## FAQs:
1. Q: Does MIT require SAT Subject Tests from Indian students?
   A: MIT no longer requires or considers SAT Subject Tests (they discontinued them). MIT is test-flexible, accepting SAT, ACT, or equivalent. For Indian students, a strong SAT (1550+) combined with excellent board results and a technical spike is the typical profile of admitted applicants.

2. Q: What is the minimum percentage in CBSE for MIT?
   A: MIT has no minimum percentage cutoff. However, admitted Indian students typically score 95%+ in CBSE boards, often with 98-100% in Mathematics and Science subjects. MIT evaluates grades in context. What matters most is whether you took the most rigorous courses available and excelled in them.

3. Q: Does MIT require research experience from Indian students?
   A: Research isn't formally required, but it's nearly universal among admitted Indian students. MIT values "makers" — students who build, experiment, and solve real problems. This can be formal research under a professor, independent projects, or technical competitions like Science Olympiad or IOPC. The key is demonstrating technical depth and initiative.

4. Q: What are MIT's English proficiency requirements for Indians?
   A: Indian students who study in English-medium schools generally don't need TOEFL/IELTS, but MIT may request it if your school's primary language of instruction isn't English. If required, MIT looks for TOEFL 100+ or IELTS 7.5+. Given most Blue Ocean students attend English-medium IB/CBSE schools, this is rarely an issue.
