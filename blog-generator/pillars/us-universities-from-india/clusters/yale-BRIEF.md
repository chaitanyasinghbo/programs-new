## FAQs:
1. Q: What SAT score do I need for Yale?
   A: Yale's middle 50% SAT range is 1510-1570. Test-optional is available through 2026. Submit if 1530+. Yale weighs essays and "intellectual curiosity" heavily for Indian applicants, so a lower test score with an exceptional essay and spike can still succeed.

2. Q: Does Yale give full scholarships to Indian students?
   A: Yale meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for all admitted students, including international students. The average financial aid package is approximately $65,000/year. For Indian families earning under ₹35L annually, the expected family contribution can be minimal. Yale is also need-blind for international applicants as of 2023.

3. Q: How is Yale different from Harvard for Indian applicants?
   A: Yale places greater emphasis on intellectual exploration and interdisciplinary interests. Harvard tends to favour students with a strong public leadership narrative. Yale attracts students who are deeply curious across fields. If your spike involves connecting two unrelated disciplines (like economics and theatre, or biology and poetry), Yale may be a better fit.

4. Q: Does Yale have interviews for Indian applicants?
   A: Yale offers optional alumni interviews, typically conducted by Yale alumni in India via video call. While optional, doing the interview shows demonstrated interest and gives you a chance to convey personality that essays can't capture. Prepare by being ready to discuss your intellectual interests conversationally, not rehearsed.
