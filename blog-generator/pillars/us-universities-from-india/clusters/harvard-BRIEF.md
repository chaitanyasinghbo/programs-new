## FAQs:
1. Q: What percentage of Indian students get into Harvard?
   A: Harvard doesn't publish India-specific acceptance rates, but the overall international acceptance rate is approximately 3%. With roughly 1,500-2,000 Indian applicants each year and only 15-25 offers, the effective rate for Indian students is around 1-2%. The students who get in aren't just academically strong. They have a distinctive spike that makes them stand out in a pool of high-achievers.

2. Q: Does Harvard give scholarships to Indian students?
   A: Yes. Harvard meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for all admitted students, including international students. Families earning below approximately $85,000 (₹70L) pay nothing. Blue Ocean students from India have received financial aid packages worth ₹80L-₹1.2 Cr. Harvard's admissions process is need-blind for international applicants, meaning your financial need does not affect your chances of admission.

3. Q: What SAT score do I need for Harvard from India?
   A: Harvard is test-optional through the Class of 2030. The middle 50% SAT range for admitted students is 1510-1580. If your score is 1550+, submit it. If below 1500, go test-optional as it won't help your candidacy. Your extracurricular spike and essays carry more weight than your test score at Harvard.

4. Q: Can I get into Harvard with a CBSE or ICSE background?
   A: Absolutely. Harvard regularly admits students from CBSE and ICSE boards. They evaluate your grades in the context of your school's grading system and the courses available to you. Most admitted Indian students score 95%+ in their boards. If your school offers AP or IB courses, taking them signals academic ambition, but they're not required.
