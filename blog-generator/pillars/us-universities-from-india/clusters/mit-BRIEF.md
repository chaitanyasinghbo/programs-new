## FAQs:
1. Q: How many Indian students does MIT accept each year?
   A: MIT typically admits 15-20 Indian students from a pool of 1,500+ Indian applicants each year. The effective acceptance rate for Indian students is roughly 1-1.5%. MIT looks for what they call "match" - students who will thrive in MIT's intense, collaborative, hands-on environment. Strong academics alone aren't enough. You need demonstrated technical ability through research, projects, or competitions.

2. Q: Does MIT give financial aid to Indian students?
   A: Yes. MIT is one of only a handful of US universities that is need-blind for international students and meets 100% of demonstrated need. If you get in, MIT will fund you regardless of your family's financial situation. Blue Ocean students have received MIT financial aid packages ranging from ₹80L to ₹1.2 Cr over four years.

3. Q: Is MIT harder to get into than Harvard for Indian students?
   A: They're comparably selective (both around 3-4% overall), but they look for different things. Harvard values well-rounded leadership and narrative. MIT values technical depth, research ability, and "maker" mentality. Indian students with STEM competition wins or original research often have a stronger shot at MIT than Harvard. The application strategy should be different for each.

4. Q: What extracurriculars does MIT want from Indian students?
   A: MIT values technical depth over breadth. Winning Science Olympiad or IOPC matters more than being school president. They want to see projects you've built, research you've conducted, problems you've solved. One student we guided to MIT had built an air quality monitoring network across 15 schools in Delhi. That's the kind of initiative MIT looks for — technical skill applied to real problems.
