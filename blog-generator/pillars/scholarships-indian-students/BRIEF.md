## FAQs:
1. Q: Can Indian students get 100% scholarships to study abroad?
   A: Yes. Five US universities (Harvard, MIT, Princeton, Yale, Amherst) are need-blind for international students and meet 100% of demonstrated need. This means if you get admitted and your family can't afford the full cost, they cover the gap completely. Blue Ocean students have received packages covering 90-100% of total costs, averaging ₹1 Crore over 4 years.

2. Q: What is the easiest scholarship for Indian students to get?
   A: "Easiest" is relative, but the Narotam Sekhsaria Foundation and Ratan Tata Scholarship have broader eligibility criteria and higher acceptance rates than Ivy League financial aid. For university-specific aid, merit scholarships at top 20-50 ranked US schools (like Emory, NYU, USC) are more achievable than Ivy League need-based aid. The strategy is applying to a mix of reach and target schools with strong scholarship programs.

3. Q: When should I start applying for scholarships?
   A: Start researching in Grade 10 and apply alongside your university applications in Grade 12. Most US university financial aid applications (CSS Profile) are due in January-February. External scholarships like Inlaks and Commonwealth have separate timelines, typically March-June for the following academic year. The scholarship calendar in this guide covers every major deadline.

4. Q: Do scholarships cover living expenses or just tuition?
   A: It depends on the scholarship. Harvard, MIT, Princeton, and Yale's financial aid covers tuition, room, board, books, and even travel. The Inlaks Scholarship covers tuition plus a living allowance. The Narotam Sekhsaria Foundation provides a loan-scholarship hybrid. Read each scholarship's terms carefully. Blue Ocean helps families build a financial strategy that combines university aid with external scholarships for maximum coverage.
