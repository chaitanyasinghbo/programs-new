Against the Odds

Below avg GPA to Ivy scholarships

From below-average GPA and test scores to offers from Cornell and Johns Hopkins, Tathagata proved that direction and clarity can outweigh early numbers.

TathagataCornell '28Johns Hopkins

He grew up discussing finance strategy at home and developed a serious interest in fintech. That interest became tangible through a startup research paper and focused project work.

Instead of hiding weaker parts of the profile, we built an honest narrative around growth, conviction, and fit. His essays explained exactly why Cornell's financial innovation ecosystem and Hopkins' interdisciplinary structure matched his goals.

The final application felt coherent, intentional, and credible. Admissions teams saw not just potential, but execution.

Small Town to Ivy

From a small village to Ivy League

From Bihar to admits at Brown and Carnegie Mellon University, Manya turned lived experience into a powerful design-for-impact portfolio.

ManyaBrown '28Carnegie Mellon

Growing up between her village and urban spaces, she saw opportunity gaps for women and people with special needs. Her work was not theoretical. It was lived and field-tested.

At Equity Foundation, she designed trust-building tools that improved group participation. Through Mitti Social Initiatives, she launched an accessible e-commerce platform for products from 50 NGOs. With Microsoft's Ludic Design Lab, she helped shape adaptive concepts into usable products.

Our role was to connect these experiences into a single design-and-technology mission. The final profile matched exactly what top innovation programs value.

Core Shift
Many initiatives to one cohesive social impact direction
Outcome
Brown + CMU admits with mission-fit positioning
